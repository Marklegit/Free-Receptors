Understanding Opioids

There are different categories within opioids.

  • Opioids refers to a class of drugs that are made from the opium poppy plants. They are often prescribed for treating pain. Another name for opioid is pain killers.
  • Opiate are natural or slightly modified components of opium such as codeine and heroin.
  • Narcotics are drugs with mind-altering compound with sleep-inducing properties. They relieve pain and dull senses.

Non-addictive prescription medication

    Naloxone

  • Naloxone is used to counteract life-threatening depression of the central nervous system and respiratory system. It allows the overdose victim to breath normally.
  • Naloxone may be injected in muscles, veins, and under. It has less concentration. (0.4mg/1mL)
  • Naloxone Nasal, sprayed up the nose. (2mg/ 2mL)
  • Naloxone ONLY works if there’s opioids in the person’s system.
  • Naloxone: Last 20-90 minutes
  • Naloxone: May cause withdrawal symptoms: Vomiting, nausea, etc...
